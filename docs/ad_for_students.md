Agentic AI for Interactive Media Creation

Interactive Media (IM) production draws on a wide range of tightly interlinked skills, including design, engineering, and writing. Almost no-one is equally strong across all of them. As humans we specialise around what we love and where our talent sits, and the rest we muddle through, collaborate on, or avoid.

Enter *agentic AI*. Unlike earlier AI assistants that behaved as autocompletes or chatbots, agentic systems now plan, act, and iterate across every one of these specialisms - sketching interfaces, generating assets, writing and refactoring code, drafting and editing copy, critiquing work, following multi-step tasks with minimal hand-holding. A design collaborator, a code buddy, a content producer, a technical peer to think out loud with - on tap.

The implications are significant and reach into every part of how we work - how Interactive Media is produced, how we learn new skills, how careers develop, how teams are formed, and how we create.

This presentation is about exactly that: what agentic AI is really doing in IM production today, what's real and what's still hype, and how to work with these tools effectively *and responsibly* - where the ethical lines sit and what counts as right use, in your project work now and in the industry next.
